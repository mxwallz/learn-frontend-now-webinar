What this training covers

60 minutes. Three sections worth protecting.

01

Why you're not getting hired. Your portfolio is signalling "beginner" to every hiring manager who opens it — and it's not because the code is bad. It's because the projects are wrong. A weather app, a to-do list, a calculator: these answer the hiring manager's question ("can this person build what I need right now?") with no. Harry covers exactly what that looks like and why it's costing most people callbacks before anyone reads a line of their code.

02

What actually gets you hired — including a live portfolio review. Industry-specific projects prove you understand the domain and can contribute from day one. Generic clones prove you can follow a tutorial. Harry pulls up real portfolios live, walks through what's wrong, what it signals, and what a strong one looks like by comparison. Before and after, in real time. If you submit yours via the form below, it may be one of them.

03

Why right now is the window. The junior frontend market is the best it's been in 3 years. Mass layoffs hit mid and senior levels hardest — companies are now rebuilding junior teams. March and April are peak hiring season. After that it slows. Harry covers the data, the placement results, and what needs to be in place in the next 60 days to catch this cycle rather than sit it out.

You're also walking away with

12 specific principles for structuring your portfolio, GitHub, and resume — the concrete framework Harry uses when reviewing applications. That's the takeaway regardless of where you're starting from.
